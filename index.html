How It Works

💊

Drug Encoding

The SMILES string is converted to a 1024-bit Morgan fingerprint (radius 2) via RDKit, then passed through a linear layer to produce a 256-dimensional drug feature vector.

→
🧬

Protein Encoding

Each amino acid is mapped to an integer and embedded into a 128-dimensional space. A 2-layer Transformer Encoder with 4 attention heads captures long-range dependencies across up to 512 residues.

→
🔗

Fusion & Prediction

An attention layer summarises the protein features. The drug and protein vectors are concatenated (256 + 128 = 384-d), passed through two fully-connected layers, and output a sigmoid probability.

→
📊

ADMET Analysis

Lipinski descriptors (MolWt, LogP, H-donors, H-acceptors) are computed with RDKit to assess drug-likeness, BBB permeability, and heuristic toxicity.
